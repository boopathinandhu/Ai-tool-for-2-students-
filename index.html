How students can use this website

Use this simple 3-step method to move from confusion to clarity.

Step 1: Know Yourself

Ask what subjects you like most, whether you enjoy coding, drawing, machines, buildings, electronics, biology or problem solving.

Step 2: Compare Courses

Open the Engineering Courses tab and compare syllabus overview, jobs, companies and government opportunities.

Step 3: Use AI Tools Smartly

Go to the AI Tools tab and use the step-by-step prompts to revise chapters, improve understanding and plan your next exams.

Do

  • Use AI for explanation and practice
  • Cross-check important facts with teachers/books
  • Use your own notes for revision

Avoid

  • Do not copy answers blindly
  • Do not trust every output without checking
  • Do not choose a course only because friends choose it

Guidance for parents

Parents play a major role in course selection. The best decision is taken when student interest, affordability, travel, college quality and job scope are considered together.

Check Interest

A student who likes computers may do better in CSE/IT/AI. A student who likes buildings and field work may fit Civil.

Check College Quality

See faculty, labs, placement support, training, discipline and learning environment before deciding.

Check Long-Term Value

Focus on whether the course gives durable skills and allows both private and government job options.
